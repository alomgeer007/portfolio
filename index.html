    Projects

    CKD Prediction

    This study explores machine learning approaches for early diagnosis of chronic kidney disease (CKD). Predictive modeling was applied to identify key data factors, narrowing down 25 variables to 30%. Twelve classifiers were tested, with XgBoost achieving the highest accuracy (0.983). The research highlights machine learning’s potential in improving CKD prediction accuracy.

    Review Paper

    This review examines Machine Learning (ML) and Deep Learning (DL) techniques for improving Parkinson's Disease (PD) diagnosis using voice, handwriting, and wave spiral datasets. It analyzes various ML and DL algorithms, highlighting their effectiveness in identifying biomarkers, enhancing diagnostic accuracy, and aiding clinical decision-making.

    Tiredness Measurement

    This paper uses computer vision to detect driver tiredness in real time, helping prevent accidents. The Haar-cascade classifier identifies whether eyes are open or closed. Focusing on the right eye to reduce processing, the system captures webcam frames and triggers alerts when eyes remain closed, delivering satisfactory results.
